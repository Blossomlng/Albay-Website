Albay is a province in the Bicol area that is well-known for the Mayon volcano. The first thing that comes to mind when we hear Albay is the Mayon volcano, but the province has enough biodiversity to provide tourists with a long list of tourist attractions. Beautiful mountain peaks, lush hills, majestic waterfalls, nature parks, and virgin islands are just a few of the province's tourist attractions.

MAYON
VOLCANO
The Mayon Volcano, which stands at 2,463 meters above sea level and is considered the world's perfect conical shape volcano, is one of the key reasons why Albay is a well-known province in the Philippines, but keep in mind that it is also the Philippines' most active volcano
PINAMUNTUGAN
ISLAND
The Pinamuntugan Island is covered in green trees in the center, and its coasts offer a very long stretch of pristine white sand beach, cove, rock formations, and turquoise sea water. It is rich in different kinds of fish and corals, making it a wonderful snorkeling spot.
CAGSAWA
RUINS
The Cagsawa ruins were formerly a church that was built in 1587 and subsequently destroyed in 1636 by Dutch pirates. In 1587, it was repaired by a priest, but in 1814, the historic eruption of Mayon Volcano devastated the church and the village of Cagsawa once again.
Hoyopan Cave
"Hoyop” means “blowing of air“, hence the name Hoyop-Hoyopan Cave covers 31.4 square meters of land area and has many entrances and exit points. The numerous openings make the gentle winds blow inside the cave providing natural ventilation.
