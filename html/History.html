MAYON

VOLCANO

Mayon
Volcano

Mayon volcano (also known as Bulkang Magayon, which means beautiful volcano), located in Legazpi, Albay. Is a major tourist attraction of the province, known for its symmetrical, almost perfect cone that rises over 7,500 feet above sea level. Its lava buried the town and church of Cagsawa. The name Mayon is said to come from a well-known folktale about Princess Daragang Magayon (name translates to “beautiful maiden”).

Eruption
of Mayon
Volcano

The most destructive eruption of the Mayon Volcano was recorded in 1814, which destroyed dozens of villages with the overwhelming output of volcanic lava and hot gas. The eruption was so strong that it contributed to what they called the 1816 “Year without a Summer”, along with the explosion of Mount Tambora in Indonesia in 1815.

Casualty
of the
Eruption

The most remembered casualty of the 1814 eruption was the Cagsawa Church. Due to the abrupt nature of the eruption, hundreds were forced to take refuge within the closed doors of the convent. The lava stream and accompanying ash proved to be too much and engulfed the church where it stood. The site remains one of the most picturesque vantage points of Mayon today. The blackened ruins of the church tower stand tall to this day, serving as a reminder of both the beauty and calamity that surrounds it.
