FESTIVALS

Magayon
Festival

Magayon Festival means "Lovely”. the most known and Celebrated festival in the capital of Albay, Magayon Festival originates from the popular legend of Mayon Volcano in honor of the province’s bounty and beauty. In remembrance of the elegance, wealth, abundant harvest, and enduring culture that exists inside their hearts this festival is celebrated for the entire month of May. The stage reenactment of the sad love tale of Daragang Magayon and Panganoron, whose untimely deaths gave rise to the volcano, is one of the highlights of the festival.

Ibalong
Festival

This festival showcases the socio-historic-cultural part of The Legazpi city, Albay, it is celebrated from August 10 to 19. The festival's main attraction is a street parade about the heroic epic of Ibalong along with the three brave men Baltog, Handyong, and Bantong, as they fought against monsters and gigantic animals, and making the land peaceful by saving locals from disasters and setting laws of the land. The cultural background of Ibalong Festival shows the bravery, resilience, warmth, and hospitality of the Bicolanos.

Pinangat
Festival

Pinangat Festival is celebrated annually by the residents of Camalig Albay to commemorate a successful harvest and the town's great cuisine. THe Pinangat festival's name comes from the popular Filipino meal "pinangat," which is cooked with coconut milk and taro leaves. The pinangat festival is celebrated Every month of June, in conjunction with the municipal fiesta, in honor of St. John the Baptist, the patron saint of the town. The Guiness Book of World Records just recognized this celebration as the world's longest pinangat boodle Fight, which took place along the roads of Camalig, Albay.

TRADITION

Pantomina

Pantomina is a Bicolano folk dance performed primarily for courtship and wedding dance. It is considered as one of the distinct cultural practices in the Bicol region. Its movements are indicative of the patronizing love making of doves. This truly exemplifies how the Bicolanos preserve and promote their illustrious dance tradition.

Pamalay

A lovely and old-fashioned tradition of bringing the bride and groom's families together. Before they officially tie the knot, the man's family will formally ask the woman's family. In this way, they will be able to talk about their future plans and eventually seek advice from the elders in order to maintain a positive relationship. This demonstrates how the Bicolanos were truly a family-oriented society, as depicted in the Philippines.

Tigsikan

Bicolanos are a truly artistic people; if Tagalogs have Balagtasan as a literary art form, Bicolanos have Tigsikan. This type of literacy is comparable to Japanese haiku, although, this does not follow the pattern of haiku yet it also has three phases. Interestingly, tigsikan is only used for entertainment during special occasions. Even though time has eroded its value, bicolanos continue to preserve their literature.
