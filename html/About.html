Why Albay?

White Beach in Boracay, Chocolate Hills in Bohol and Underground River in Puerto Princesa. These are the some of the famous attractions in the Philippines that both foreigners and Filipinos usually go. However, there is a Province in the Bicol Region that would leave you in awe for its beauty and diversity of tourist attractions. From volcanoes, beaches to caves and rivers, this province would surely amaze you. The province of Albay is located in southeastern Luzon. DID YOU KNOW?

With a total land area of 2, 554.06 km2, Albay is mostly mountainous with some fertile valleys and plains. Highest elevation of 2,462 meters was achieved by the Mayon Volcano. However, there are also other mountains like Mt. Malinao and Mt. Masaraga. In addition, there are hills like Buhawi of Pio and Kawa-Kawa Hill of Ligao.

DID YOU KNOW?

In the year 2021, the whole province generated revenue of 5,118,237,637.56. Its capital, Legazpi City, generated the highest score percentage with 21.9%. The major industry in Albay is agriculture. They produce abaca, coconut, rice, and sugar. Handicrafts and manufactured products from abaca contribute greatly to its economy and this includes, hats, bags, mats, hemp, and many more. Other sources include forestry, cement, and papermaking.
